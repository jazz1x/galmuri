# Socratic Probe — Questions Template

> N units × 3 axes (Definition/Difference/Attribution) = 3N questions
> (For deck presets: N = core_length + 2; for other adapters: N = units.length)

## Validation Logic

Each question is judged on two axes:

- **answerable**: Can this be answered from the claim/unit text alone? (Y/N)
- **explicit**: Is the answer literally written in the claim/unit? (Y/N)

**Pass condition**: `answerable=Y AND explicit=Y`.

**Recovery on fail**:

| Failed axis | Return to step |
|---|---|
| Definition fail | Step 2 (Decomposition) |
| Difference fail | Step 3 (Essence) |
| Attribution fail | Step 5 (Reconstruction) |

---

## Unit · Problem

- **Q1 Definition**: What is the one-line core question of this claim/unit?
- **Q2 Difference**: Is the choice being made X or Y right now?
- **Q3 Attribution**: Who owns this decision?

## Unit · Concept

- **Q1 Definition**: Define role A in one sentence.
- **Q2 Difference**: Where is the boundary between A and B?
- **Q3 Attribution**: Is this action an A or a B?

## Unit · Comparison

- **Q1 Definition**: What is the weight axis?
- **Q2 Difference**: What is the real difference between options 1 and 2?
- **Q3 Attribution**: On this axis, which side do we value more?

## Unit · Examples

- **Q1 Definition**: How is the decomposition applied in this claim/unit?
- **Q2 Difference**: Does this claim/unit share structure with the others?
- **Q3 Attribution**: In this claim/unit, who executes?

## Unit · Trade-off

- **Q1 Definition**: What do we lose?
- **Q2 Difference**: Does one side's strength become the other's weakness?
- **Q3 Attribution**: What are we willing to concede?

## Unit · Conclusion

- **Q1 Definition**: What is the conclusion in one sentence?
- **Q2 Difference**: What distinguishes this from the alternative?
- **Q3 Attribution**: Who owns Recovery?
